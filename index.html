About me

Hi, I'm Jack, a London-based designer and video editor who can work independently, as well as part of a team. I'm currently learning software development. I have an organised and proactive approach to work.

Why code?

Born in 1996, I grew up on the internet and was just old enough to appreciate the seismic shift to mobile touchscreens. In the "There's an app for that" boom that followed, I had an idea for an app that wakes you up when you're nearly at your bus stop/train station, and set about trying to learn how to use Xcode. I quickly realised I couldn't build an app in the space of a school summer holiday and gave up. I decided that coding was just too difficult for me. In the time since, I've continued to love using computers - whether that be for graphic design or video editing, and more recently, for setting up a small media server. Being introduced to Plex and Jellyfin opened up a lot of doors in terms of what technology can do. I was suddenly free of the gated and restricted access to the internet via social media, Amazon and Netflix. This discovery made me excited to find out more about open source software on GitHub. Website design and the growth of the internet has always been an interest of mine but stumbling upon Web Design as Architecture proved a significant inspiration in me wanting to learn to code. In my graphic design work, I enjoyed using CMS like Squarespace but - on top of increasingly exorbitant monthly fees - I always felt like this process was missing adaptability. On top of this, I knew these companies were complicit in centralising access to the internet. If we want to build an accessible and decolonised internet, we are going to have to build it ourselves. Just as in the poster, "Everything is architecture, everyone an architect", open source provides access to these tools for everyone who needs it. This accessibility is easier to reach than it seems. For example, keeping track of my changes on GitHub provides other people resources to learn in the future, while my profile also links to my favourite learning resources and my coding progress, to provide a loose guide, or inspire other learners.
